Personal statement
After gaining my primary medical degree (MBBS), I was working as Residential medical officer and General practitioner for around 2 years in India. After that, I passed post graduate diploma in orthopaedics (D-Ortho) from India. Following that, I completed diploma of national board of examinations (DNB Ortho) course from India and completed the thesis on “Review of clinical features and surgical analysis of 120 cases of lumber discectomy’’. I also completed I.C.U. training for two months. Then I did international fellowship (FASIF) in trauma and completed World orthopaedic education programme (WOEP) from Switzerland and a course on AO principles from Germany.
After that, I was working as Orthopaedic Surgeon in Kerala, India. I have 26 years’ experience as medical doctor, 22 years’ experience as orthopaedic Surgeon in India, experience of carrying out almost all different types of orthopaedic procedures and major surgeries, experience of working in emergency rooms, outpatient clinics, wards, minor and major operation theatres, I.C.U., labour camps and as a family doctor of medium or large multi-specialty private hospitals and clinics and government medical college as a teaching staff. My duties also included initial reception of patients presenting to emergency, resuscitate and stabilize them. I had been doing and is experienced and well versed, in all common orthopaedic traumatic and non-traumatic surgical procedures including plating, EXFIX, nailing, hemiarthroplasty, tumour surgery, osteomyelitis etc. I have taken additional training in arthroscopy, arthroplasty, paediatric surgery, pelvic surgery, hand surgery, plastic surgery and spine surgery and has some experience.
Presently I work as a Registrar (senior clinical fellow), at Major trauma department of Queens medical canter, Nottingham University Trust, NHS, UK from October 2017 onwards. such as neurosurgeon, orthopaedic surgeon, plastic surgeon etc., activating major haemorrhage protocol and organizing ward admission or critical care admission, surgery etc. I was also managing ward patients in major trauma wards, major trauma patients in trauma ICU and HDU, as per the direction of consultants, co-coordinating and liaising with around 30 different specialties related with major trauma, major trauma consultants, ED, trauma ICU and HDU staff, rehabilitation team including consultant, physiotherapists and occupational therapists, junior clinical fellows and para medical staff including pain team, critical care outreach team, nutritionists, speech therapists, EXFIX care nurse, pharmacists etc. My duties also included, teaching medical students and supervising junior clinical fellow I was also doing, secondary and tertiary surveys in the ward, patient progress review, ward medical and surgical procedures and co-operating with the audits and research programmes. My duties also included, attendance of daily major trauma and orthopaedic and trauma combined meetings and major trauma board and presenting and discussing the cases. I was also attending other meetings, including mortality morbidity meetings. I was assisting the major trauma consultants during rounds, at major trauma wards, ICU, HDU and out-layers. My duties also included, obtaining patient consents and discussing with by standers, ordering investigations, writing medications and treatment orders, retrieving reports, preparing TTOs and discharge summaries, through trust web sites and documentations in different forms and patient case charts.

The site was created in such a way that just by looking at the home page of site, it was possible to know: what are the free medical and orthopedic or medical information headings
available in net. The site was also acting as semi-official site for Kerala orthopedic association. The URL of the site was www.keralaorthopaedicsinternational.tk. Later the group migrated to ‘multiply’ and the present URLs are keralaorthopaedics.multiply.com and keralaorthopaedics international.org. Dr. Jimmy Acted as manager of Kerala orthopedics international web site from 2003 onwards. These web sites contain many accident care links which are useful to general public.

	•	Official site and I.T. Secretary post
Because of the inspiration obtained by the semi-official site of Dr. Jimmy M. L. Kerala orthopedic association allowed Dr. Jimmy M. L. to create a new official web site for Kerala orthopedic association and the URL of the site was www.keralaorthopaedicassociation.tk at 2005. A new post named state I.T.Secretary post was created by the association in 2005 & Dr. Jimmy acted as I.T.Secretary for 5 years. The site was linked to ‘Kerala orthopedics international’ site with 2000 links, KOA discussion site, notification area and ‘clinical research instruments’ sub site. The site has many accident care links, official files and photos and seminar reports. The ‘clinical research instruments’ sub site is useful for research work. The site was later improvised and the URL was changed (2007 to 2010) to keralaorthopaedicassociation.org.

	•	Web site upgradation in 2011
A new personal web site of Dr. Jimmy M.L. was created www.doctorjimmy.net (old site was www.doctorjimmy.tk). The site contains links to face book, articles, seminar reports, consultation times and other important sites managed by Dr. Jimmy M. L.
